This unit builds upon Unit 6 by exploring advanced topics in negligence law, including professional negligence and negligent misstatement. You will learn about pure economic loss, special duties of care, and important defenses.

本单元在第六单元的基础上，探讨疏忽法的高级主题，包括专业疏忽和疏忽误述。您将学习纯经济损失、特殊注意义务和重要的抗辩理由。

Topics Covered 涵盖主题

1. Professional Negligence 专业疏忽

Definition: Professional negligence occurs when a person providing professional services fails to exercise the degree of skill and care that would be expected of a reasonably competent member of that profession.

专业疏忽是指提供专业服务的人未能行使该专业中合理能干的成员所期望的技能和注意程度。

Professional Standards 专业标准
  • Medical practitioners: Must possess and exercise reasonable skill expected of a medical practitioner 医疗从业者必须具备并运用医疗从业者应有的合理技能
  • Legal practitioners: Must provide advice and services with reasonable competence 法律从业者必须以合理能力提供建议和服务
  • Accountants and auditors: Must exercise professional skill and care in financial advice and reporting 会计师和审计师必须在财务建议和报告中运用专业技能和谨慎
  • Engineers and architects: Must design and supervise with appropriate technical expertise 工程师和建筑师必须以适当的技术专长进行设计和监督
Key Cases 关键案例

Rogers v Whitaker (1992) 175 CLR 479:

  • Facts: Eye surgeon failed to warn patient of rare risk of sympathetic ophthalmia
  • Held: Doctor must warn patient of material risks - those a reasonable person in the patient's position would attach significance to
  • Principle: Professional standard includes duty to warn of material risks 专业标准包括警告实质风险的义务
Civil Liability Act Provisions s5O 民事责任法第5O条

A professional is not negligent if they acted in a manner widely accepted by peer professional opinion as competent professional practice (Bolam test modified).

如果专业人士以同行专业意见广泛接受的方式作为合格的专业实践(修改的Bolam测试)，则不构成疏忽。

2. Negligent Misstatement 疏忽误述

Definition: Negligent misstatement occurs when a person makes a careless statement that causes economic loss to someone who reasonably relies on it.

疏忽误述是指一个人做出疏忽的陈述，给合理依赖该陈述的人造成经济损失。

Leading Case: Hedley Byrne v Heller (1964) AC 465 关键案例

Facts: Advertising agency relied on bank reference about client's creditworthiness. Bank gave favorable but inaccurate reference. Client went bankrupt, causing loss to agency.

Held: Duty of care can arise for negligent statements where there is a "special relationship" between parties. However, bank protected by disclaimer.

Principle Established:

  • Special relationship required for duty of care in misstatement 误述中的注意义务需要特殊关系
  • Person making statement must have special skill/knowledge 做出陈述的人必须具有特殊技能/知识
  • Statement maker knows or ought to know of reliance 陈述者知道或应该知道依赖
  • Reasonable for recipient to rely on statement 接受者依赖陈述是合理的
Elements of Negligent Misstatement 疏忽误述的要素
  1. Special relationship: Between statement maker and recipient 陈述者和接受者之间的特殊关系
  2. Special skill: Statement maker possesses special skill or knowledge 陈述者拥有特殊技能或知识
  3. Known reliance: Statement maker knows statement will be relied upon 陈述者知道陈述将被依赖
  4. Reasonable reliance: Recipient's reliance is reasonable in circumstances 接受者的依赖在情况下是合理的
  5. Breach: Statement made carelessly or without reasonable basis 陈述是疏忽地或没有合理依据做出的
  6. Causation: Reliance on statement caused the loss 对陈述的依赖造成了损失
Australian Cases 澳大利亚案例

Shaddock & Associates v Parramatta CC (1981) 150 CLR 225:

  • Council gave inaccurate information about road widening proposal
  • Purchaser relied on information when buying property
  • Council held liable for negligent misstatement
  • Principle: Public authorities can owe duty of care for advice/information 公共机构可能对建议/信息负有注意义务

San Sebastian Pty Ltd v Minister (1986) 162 CLR 340:

  • Government Minister's department gave inaccurate advice
  • Company suffered loss relying on advice
  • Principle: Duty of care exists when public authority exercises special skill/knowledge 当公共机构行使特殊技能/知识时存在注意义务

3. Pure Economic Loss 纯经济损失

Definition: Financial loss that is not consequent upon physical injury to person or property damage. Generally more difficult to recover than physical damage.

不是由人身伤害或财产损害引起的经济损失。通常比物理损害更难获得赔偿。

Categories of Pure Economic Loss 纯经济损失的类别
  1. Negligent misstatement: Loss from relying on careless advice 因依赖疏忽建议而造成的损失
  2. Defective products/buildings: Economic loss from defects (limited recovery) 因缺陷造成的经济损失(有限赔偿)
  3. Relational economic loss: Loss from damage to third party's property 因第三方财产损害造成的损失
  4. Loss of expected financial advantage: Failed business opportunities 失去的商业机会
General Rule: Pure Economic Loss Not Recoverable 一般规则：纯经济损失不可追回

Reasons for restrictive approach:

  • Fear of indeterminate liability (too many potential plaintiffs)
  • Difficulty in determining scope of duty
  • Economic losses often spread more widely than physical damage
  • Contract law better suited for commercial relationships
Exceptions: When Pure Economic Loss IS Recoverable 例外：何时可以追回纯经济损失
  1. Negligent misstatement (Hedley Byrne principle)
  2. Assumption of responsibility for specific task
  3. Special relationships with known reliance
  4. Defective products causing danger (limited - mainly contract law)

4. Special Relationships and Reliance 特殊关系与信赖

What Constitutes a Special Relationship? 什么构成特殊关系？

A special relationship exists when:

  • Expertise: Statement maker has special skill, knowledge, or expertise 陈述者具有特殊技能、知识或专长
  • Business/professional context: Statement made in business or professional capacity 在商业或专业能力下做出陈述
  • Known reliance: Maker knows or should know recipient will rely on statement 制造者知道或应该知道接受者将依赖陈述
  • Serious matter: Statement relates to serious matter involving potential loss 陈述涉及涉及潜在损失的严重事项
  • Reasonable reliance: Recipient's reliance is reasonable in circumstances 接受者的依赖在情况下是合理的
Types of Special Relationships 特殊关系的类型
  • Professional-Client: Doctor-patient, lawyer-client, accountant-client
  • Financial Advisor-Investor: Investment advice, credit references
  • Surveyor/Valuer-Purchaser: Property valuations and surveys
  • Public Authority-Citizen: When exercising special knowledge/expertise
  • Employer-Employee: Regarding references and employment matters
Reasonable Reliance 合理信赖

Courts consider whether it was reasonable for plaintiff to rely on statement by examining:

  • Expertise of statement maker vs recipient
  • Whether plaintiff had opportunity to verify independently
  • Nature and importance of transaction
  • Context in which statement was made
  • Any disclaimers or qualifications

5. Defenses to Negligence 疏忽的抗辩

Contributory Negligence 共同过失

Civil Liability Act s5R: Plaintiff's own carelessness contributed to harm

  • Effect: Damages reduced proportionately to plaintiff's fault 损害赔偿按原告的过错比例减少
  • Application: Court apportions responsibility (e.g., 70% defendant, 30% plaintiff)
  • Examples: Not wearing seatbelt, ignoring warnings, failing to mitigate loss

Case Example: Pennington v Norris (1956) - Passenger injured when driver negligent but passenger knew driver was drunk. Both contributed to harm.

Voluntary Assumption of Risk (Volenti) 自愿承担风险

Civil Liability Act s5L-5N: Plaintiff knowingly and voluntarily accepted risk

  • Requirements:
    • Plaintiff had knowledge of risk 原告知道风险
    • Plaintiff voluntarily accepted risk 原告自愿接受风险
    • Acceptance was with full appreciation of nature/extent of risk 充分理解风险的性质和程度
  • Effect: Complete defense - no liability 完全抗辩-无责任
  • Difficult to establish: Courts reluctant to find volenti 法院不愿认定自愿承担风险

Example: Participating in dangerous sports with full knowledge of inherent risks

Limitation Periods 时效限制

Limitation Act 1969 (NSW): Time limits for bringing legal action

  • General rule: 6 years from when cause of action arose 从诉因产生之日起6年
  • Personal injury: 3 years from date of discoverability 从可发现之日起3年
  • Extension possible: Court discretion in special circumstances 在特殊情况下法院有酌情权
  • Effect: Action barred if outside limitation period 如果超过时效期限，诉讼将被禁止
Disclaimers and Exclusion Clauses 免责声明和排除条款

Can limit or exclude liability for negligent misstatement if:

  • Clear and unambiguous wording 措辞清晰明确
  • Brought to recipient's attention 引起接受者注意
  • Reasonable in circumstances 在情况下合理
  • Not contrary to statute (e.g., unfair contract terms) 不违反法规

Example: Hedley Byrne v Heller - bank's disclaimer "without responsibility" was effective

Summary 总结

Unit 7 extends your understanding of negligence law into specialized areas. The key concepts are:

  1. Professional negligence requires higher standards based on professional expertise
  2. Negligent misstatement allows recovery for pure economic loss when special relationship exists
  3. Pure economic loss is generally not recoverable except in limited circumstances
  4. Defenses can reduce or eliminate liability for negligent conduct

第7单元将您对疏忽法的理解扩展到专业领域。关键概念是专业疏忽需要基于专业知识的更高标准，疏忽误述在存在特殊关系时允许追回纯经济损失，纯经济损失通常不可追回，除非在有限的情况下，抗辩可以减少或消除疏忽行为的责任。
