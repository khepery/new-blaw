Question 1: EPBC Act Application

Scenario: GreenMine Ltd proposes a new coal mine in Queensland, 15km from the Great Barrier Reef Marine Park. The mine will produce 5 million tonnes of coal annually for export. Environmental groups raise concerns about impacts on marine ecosystems and downstream emissions from burning the coal overseas.

Questions:

  • What matters of national environmental significance might be affected?
  • Must the Minister consider downstream (Scope 3) greenhouse gas emissions?
  • What assessment level would likely be required (EIS, PER, or preliminary)?
Question 2: Safeguard Mechanism Compliance

Scenario: SteelCorp operates a facility that emitted 150,000 tonnes CO2-e last year. Their baseline for this year is 145,000 tonnes, declining 4.9% annually. They're considering three options:

  1. Invest $5M in energy efficiency to reduce emissions to 135,000 tonnes
  2. Continue operations and buy ACCUs (current price: $35/tonne)
  3. Do nothing and face penalties

Questions:

  • Analyze the cost and compliance implications of each option
  • What are SteelCorp's legal obligations under the Safeguard Mechanism?
  • Which option provides the best long-term value? Why?
  • How do declining baselines affect the decision over the next 5-10 years?
Question 3: Greenwashing Risk Assessment

Scenario: EcoPackaging Pty Ltd manufactures plastic packaging and wants to market a new product as "100% eco-friendly and sustainable packaging." The product is 30% recycled plastic (70% virgin plastic), technically recyclable but requires label removal and cleaning (which most consumers don't do), and the company hasn't measured its carbon footprint.

Questions:

  • Identify the greenwashing risks in the proposed marketing claims
  • Which Australian Consumer Law provisions might be violated?
  • What penalties could ACCC impose for greenwashing?
  • How should EcoPackaging modify their claims to comply with ACL?
  • What evidence should they gather before making environmental claims?
Question 4: Corporate Climate Disclosure

Scenario: RetailCo is an ASX-listed retail company with 200 stores. The board is debating whether to adopt TCFD-aligned climate reporting. The CFO argues it's expensive and time-consuming. The Sustainability Manager argues it's necessary for risk management and investor expectations.

Questions:

  • What are RetailCo's current legal obligations regarding climate disclosure?
  • What are the four TCFD pillars RetailCo would need to address?
  • What are the benefits of voluntary TCFD adoption before it becomes mandatory?
  • What physical and transition climate risks might RetailCo face?
  • How might investor expectations influence this decision?
Question 5: POEO Act Pollution Incident

Scenario: A chemical manufacturer in NSW experiences a malfunction that releases toxic fumes into the air near a residential area. The release lasts 2 hours before being controlled. No immediate health impacts are reported, but air quality monitoring shows elevated pollutant levels. The company holds an EPL but the release exceeded licensed limits.

Questions:

  • What are the company's immediate legal obligations under the POEO Act?
  • Could this constitute a Tier 1 or Tier 2 offence? Explain the difference.
  • What penalties could apply?
  • What factors might influence the EPA's enforcement response?
